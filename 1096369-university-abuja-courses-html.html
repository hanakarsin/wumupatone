University of Abuja courses, fees, and requirements: 2024/2025 update

The Federal University of Abuja (UNIABUJA) was established in 1988 in Abuja, Nigeria's administrative and political capital. It is a dual-mode university for conventional and distance learning programs. Here is a list of the University of Abuja courses (undergraduate and postgraduate) and general admission requirements.

UNIABUJA, or UofA, is one of Nigeria's top federal tertiary education institutions. The school offers a wide range of courses under various faculties and departments. In addition, UofA has a students' employment scheme, an internship and linkages unit, a center for undergraduate research, and a mentorship program.

University of Abuja school fees

The current school fees of the University of Abuja, Gwagwalada is ₦61,340 for the new and ₦45,340 for old students.

How can I get admission to UNIABUJA?

Undergraduates at the 100 level should meet the following University of Abuja courses admission requirements:

  • You must score at least a credit pass in five subjects, including English Language and Mathematics (not more than two sittings).
  • Three of the credit passes must be in subjects relevant to the degree course applied.
  • You must attain the acceptable JAMB cut-off mark and UTME score.
  • You must have attained UNIABUJA's post-UTME exam (the university's departmental cut-off mark).
  • A Direct Entry (DE) admission candidate must have at least two (2) ‘A’ Level passes, or equivalent in addition to five (5) credit passes in other subjects (including English Language and Mathematics) at the ordinary level (WAEC, NECO, GCE, NABTEB and Grade II).
  • A credit pass in O'Level English Language is required for all courses.
  • A credit in Mathematics is required for all science-based courses.

Here is a list of the University of Abuja admission requirements for postgraduate studies:

  • You should have a Higher National Diploma (HND) and Bachelor’s Degree.
  • You must have credit passes in five (5) subjects, including English Language and Mathematics at the ordinary level (WAEC, NECO, GCE, NABTEB, and Grade II), and must be obtained at no more than two (2) sittings.

For admission into any UNIABUJA Ph.D. program:

  • You must have obtained a weighted average score of 60% or a letter ‘B’ grade in the Master’s degree relevant to the field of study.
  • You should have five (5) subject credit passes, including English Language and Mathematics at the ordinary level (WAEC, NECO, GCE, NABTEB, and Grade II), and must be obtained at no more than two (2) sittings.

What is the cut-off mark for all courses in the University of Abuja?

The University of Abuja courses and cut-off marks vary per faculty and department. JAMB's approved UTME cut-off mark for UNIABUJA is 180 for all admissions. However, find out UNIABUJA's post-UTME test departmental cut-off mark from the institution.

Can UNIABUJA accept the second choice?

The University of Abuja does not enroll students who picked this school as a second choice in JAMB. Nevertheless, this does not mean that all universities in Nigeria have this policy. Some allow you to choose them as your second option and will admit you if you qualify for the course you selected.

How many years to study medicine in UNIABUJA?

It takes at least six years to study Medicine and Surgery in UNIABUJA. You will study preliminary basic sciences for 12 months, spend 18 months in the pre-clinical program in basic medical sciences, and the remaining 42 months taking courses in pathology and pharmacology.

Other universities in Nigeria also apply the same curriculum to medical doctor students. After completing your medical undergraduate degree, you can take core clinical courses in medicine, obstetrics and gynecology, surgery, community medicine, and psychiatry.
